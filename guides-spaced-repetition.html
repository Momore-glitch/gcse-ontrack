Spaced Repetition for GCSE Revision

Spaced repetition helps you remember information long-term instead of forgetting it after a few days.

What is spaced repetition?

It means revising the same topic multiple times over increasing gaps.

  • Day 1 – learn the topic
  • Day 3 – quick review
  • Day 7 – test yourself
  • Day 14 – past questions

Why it works

Your brain remembers things better when it has to retrieve them after a gap.

How to use it effectively

  • Combine with active recall
  • Keep sessions short
  • Review weak topics more often

Plan your revision properly

  • Create a revision plan
  • Build a timetable
